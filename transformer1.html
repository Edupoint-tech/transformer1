Defination

Transformer is a static device that transfer electrical energy from one electrical circuit to other electrical circuit through the medium of magnetic field and without a change in frequency.

Actually transformer is a Electromagnetic energy conversion device.

Principle of transformer action:-

A transformer works on principle of Electromagnetic induction between two or more coupled circuits or coils.According to this principle ,an EMF is induced in coil if it links a changing flux

Direction of induced EMF will be according to Laurange's law.

In general,a transformer performes following tasks-

(i) Decreasin or increasing voltage and current level from one circuit to another circuit.

(ii) Matching the impedance of a source and its load for maximum power transfer in electronic and control circuits.

(iii) solating d.c. while permitting the flow of a.c. between two circuits or for isolating one circuit from another.

Winding

Transformer has two types of windings:

1.Primary winding

The electric circuit which receives electrical energy from main supply.

2.Secondary winding

The circuit which deliveres electrical energy to the load

Important Note:

(i).The primary and secondary windings are not electrically coupled.They are coupled through the medium of magnetic field

(ii).Low power transformers are air cooled.

(iii).High power transformers are oil cooled.

(iv).Transformer's core made of CRGO (cold rolled grain oriented) steel sheet to reduce core loss.
